Project Overview

From my work on Queen's Aerospace Design Team

As the Aerodynamics Lead, I designed a fixed-wing twin propellor drone using OpenVSP and QAPOT (QADT Python Optimization Script) using the AeroSandBox Library in preparation for the 2026 competition. Analysis is currently being conducted using XFLR5 to ensure stability and CFD simulations to optimize the design for lift. The drone draws inspiration from the Twin Otter and is designed for stability and banner towing capabilities. The final competition drone will be designed by sub-team members!

Design Constraints


- Wingspan: 1.5 meters (competition limit)
- Payload Capacity: 5 kg (for banner towing, rubber duck passengers, and hockey puck cargo)
- Endurance: 10 mins and 30s @ 70% throttle (competition: 5 min flight window)
- Stability: Must maintain stable flight in moderate wind conditions, steep turns and manual pilot inputs
- Structural Integrity: Must withstand forces during takeoff, flight maneuvers (orbits), and landing

CFD Analysis

CFD analysis through OpenFOAM and Paraview is conducted on the first prototype iteration. Simulation constraints include simulating it as zero angle of attack, steady state, incompressible flow at 24 m/s using simpleFoam. The mesh is generated using CFMesh with refinement around the entire body, and finer cells at the trailing edges. Computaitonal resouces are a major constraint, with only a regular desktop computer available for simulations. This dictated meshing size and complexity of the model. The trailing edges are especially important to capture the wake effects and vortices generated by the wings and control surfaces and are therefore refined using a line segment. Additionally, only half the drone is simulated using a symmetry plane to reduce computational cost. The final mesh contains just under 2 million cells, with the outermost boundary at 0.15m while smallest cells around the body are 0.001m. The mesh passes all quality checks in OpenFoam except for 6 open cells. Post processing using Paraview visualizes turbulent kinetic energy, pressure distribution around the drone. (Reflect, threshold, contour, streamlines etc.) Numerically, the lift and drag coefficients are similar with an error around 20% when compared with QAPOT (Python script) results. The results will be used to optimize the design in its second iteration.
