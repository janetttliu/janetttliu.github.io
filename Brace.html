Exoskeleton Thigh Brace

From my work on the Queen's Biomechatronics Design Team.

Project Overview

During my first year on the team I helped design this exoskeleton thigh brace which won first place at the ACE exoskeleton competition in Michigan last year.

Design Constraints


The exoskeleton was deisgned to be a hard lower body exoskeleton that would help first responders. It had to be easy to take off, not too bulky, able to resist decent impact, comfortable, and allow for as much movement as possible. Taking these constaints into consideration I made a thigh brace which did not wrap around the entire leg, but instead only went halfway. This provided enough solid support, and could be connected with the use of a velcro strap. That velcro strap would also make the brace easier to take off. The large hole on the side was designed to be able to connect to the main frame which was carbon fiber tubing. Furthermore, the edges were all fileted to make it more comfortable. Eventually the model was 3D printed horizontally as well to strengthen the overall joint. Having the print lines go across horizontally make the brace less prone to breaking.
